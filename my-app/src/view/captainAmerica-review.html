The Death of Captain America Volume One: The Death of The Dream Review

Before I give my opinions of this volume, I must say that I was never really into Captain America. I knew of his death in comics, as well as his replacement by his former partner Bucky Barnes. I was also aware of his inevitable return to comics. It was this death that caused me to research his lore and why Steve Rogers became the inspirational Captain America.

I greatly enjoyed this volume. I chose a bad part to dive into the series, but I knew enough of Ed Brubaker's Captain America run to become fully immersed in this world. I could not stop reading this book. This may have been the fastest that I have ever read a volume because it was that engaging. Ed Brubaker definitely knows how to create a plot intricate enough to engage new readers, while creating a sense of urgency and panic when various different events occur.

This book greatly impressed me. I did not know what to expect when I picked up this trade paperback, and I am greatly pleased with my purchase. As of writing this, I am currently reading Captain America: Red Menace, and I am highly anticipating Volume 2 of The Death of Captain America.
